# Sustain — Brand & Design Brief

## Overview
Sustain is a native macOS worship-leading utility for pads, click, count-off, and smooth song transitions. The product should feel calm, reliable, and premium — more like an Apple utility than church software or audio-production software.

## Core Brand Positioning
Sustain is for worship leaders who need:
- confidence under pressure
- seamless transitions
- minimal cognitive load
- calm, trustworthy tools

The app should not look like:
- church management software
- a DAW
- EDM / audio-visualizer software
- loud volunteer tools

It should feel closer to:
- Things 3
- Bear
- Apple native apps
- Fujifilm / Muji / Fellow-style restraint

## Name
**Sustain**

Why it won:
- It communicates the outcome, not just the feature.
- It feels musical without being too technical.
- It sounds natural in use: “Open Sustain,” “Set the key in Sustain,” “Start Sustain after prayer.”
- It is broad enough to grow with the product.

## Tagline
**Atmosphere. Presence. Flow.**

This summarizes the emotional and functional experience:
- Atmosphere: the sonic space
- Presence: the feeling pads create
- Flow: the seamless movement between moments

## Final Logo Direction
### Chosen concept
**Single flowing wave + subtle ripple resonance field**

### Meaning
- **Wave** = sustained sound
- **Ripple field** = resonant space / sound filling the room
- **Combined** = seamless worship flow

### Important decisions
- The **wave is the hero**.
- The **ripple field is supporting**.
- The wave should be rendered as **one stroke only**.
- No inner highlight, inner border, bevel, or ribbon-within-ribbon effect.
- The wave should feel singular, calm, and memorable.
- Ripple lines should be subtle, organic, and slightly imperfect.
- They should not feel technical, sonar-like, or mathematically perfect.
- The identity should communicate **presence**, not “audio software.”

## Visual Language
### Brand attributes
- Calm
- Intentional
- Organic
- Premium
- Minimal
- Native
- Reliable
- Atmospheric

### Tone
The brand should whisper, not shout.

### Avoid
- Purple “AI” gradients
- Neon
- Equalizer bars
- Music notes
- Crosses, guitars, and church clichés
- EDM / DJ aesthetics
- Busy atmosphere or particle overload

## Color Palette
### Primary
- Near Black — `#0E0F10`
- Charcoal — `#171917`
- Deep Olive — `#1E231E`
- Moss — `#3A4A39`
- Sage — `#A8BE9A`
- Warm Ivory — `#F1EFE6`
- Muted Gold — `#DCCB8A` (secondary / sparing use)

### Usage guidance
- Default icon: dark background + sage / ivory wave
- Light variant: warm ivory background + deep olive / sage wave
- Sage is the main accent color in the product UI
- Gold is optional and should be used sparingly

## Typography
Use system typography:
- SF Pro Display for large headings / marketing lockups
- SF Pro Text for interface content

Traits:
- spacious
- legible
- understated
- native to Apple platforms

## App Icon Guidance
The app icon should:
- remain legible at 32px and 16px
- prioritize the wave over the resonance field
- use the ripple field as depth, not decoration
- work in dark and light contexts
- feel premium and restrained

## Motion Guidance
Motion is part of the identity.

### Ideal animation sequence
1. The wave appears.
2. It begins to flow.
3. The ripple field gently expands.
4. The atmosphere settles.

### Motion characteristics
- subtle
- slow
- calm
- never flashy

### Product uses
- launch screen
- loading next song
- crossfade transition
- active song state
- selection changes
- onboarding / empty states

## Product UI Direction
Sustain should feel native to macOS:
- native sidebars
- native toolbars
- native sheets
- native typography
- system materials where helpful

The UI should prioritize clarity and reliability over stylistic flourish.

### Principles
- Make the current state obvious.
- Keep rehearsal-critical controls large and clear.
- Use brand motion and resonance sparingly to reinforce state or transition.
- Never let brand expression interfere with operation.
